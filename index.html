About

I collect what I can't keep.

I grew up between places, always packing and unpacking the same few things — my poems, my camera, my shark pillow.

This site is a garage sale of memory: every item here was once mine, or maybe still is, in a way.

I write, teach, and make small zines. I photograph on an iPhone 5c, the same one that's seen every version of me.

You can find me through my words more easily than through a map.

Class Publication (Elementary → Middle School)

Class Publication
From elementary through middle school, I ran the class publication.
Every issue was a small universe: I collected classmates' stories, poems, drawings, and essays,
edited them, and made sure they were ready to be printed and shared.
Being the editor wasn't just about organizing papers — it was about listening to voices,
preserving small sparks of creativity, and giving them a place to be seen.

Teaching

I once taught poems to children who had never written one before.
They read about stars, rain, and ghosts — and suddenly the classroom felt holy.
I didn't teach them how to write. They taught me how to listen.
